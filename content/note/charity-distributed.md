## Remote teams need more mature systems

The skill set for managing a distributed team is very different from that of an in person team (part time or full time).

- Lots can be communicated implicitly in person. Communication has to be very intentional when remote.
- Written communication at that. Itâ€™s easier to be a talking culture and get away with it when working in person.

## On permeating connection, trust, culture etc

- Team culture does not evolve organically; you have to set aside time to ask people personal questions to talk about your lives to do things that are not super workrelated.

### RTO as "Reverting to the Last Known Good State"

- People who had existing in-person bonds before the pandemic were "fine when they dispersed," but new hires struggled to build the necessary connections in a fully remote environment

* She acknowledges that some companies implementing RTO are not necessarily acting in bad faith, but are reacting to data showing a decline in employee well-being.
* The data indicates that trust has plummeted and people's sense of connectedness to their colleagues and workplace has plummeted.
* she believes achieving a "hybrid humane" model is so difficult, especially considering the controversy around Return to Office (RTO) mandates

## On down levelling, aka stepping down

For context, Charity transitioned from being CEO of honeycomb, to CTO.

> I never wanted to be CEO i did it reluctantly. One thing I learned was that I think you really want a pretty structured person to be your CEO and I am not that and I don't want to try and be that. I'm now the most senior IC in our company and I love it I love it!

She acknowledges that stepping down, even if it is what you desire, is difficult because

> we're all just we're naked monkeys, and part of being a mammal is being sensitive to hierarchy.

This makes stepping down a lot to process whether or not it's something you want.

She views intentional down leveling as a way to prioritize self-knowledge and self-awareness, noting that

> Sometimes, we don't know what we're capable of or what we truly love or what we truly don't love until we're tested at our edges

Down levelling is a natural part of the ebs & flows of this process.
