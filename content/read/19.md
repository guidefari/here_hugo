## Kent C Dodds

- [Write tests. Not too many. Mostly integration.](https://kentcdodds.com/blog/write-tests)
- [Making your UI tests resilient to change](https://kentcdodds.com/blog/making-your-ui-tests-resilient-to-change)
- [The Testing Trophy and Testing Classifications](https://kentcdodds.com/blog/the-testing-trophy-and-testing-classifications)
- [How to know what to test](https://kentcdodds.com/blog/how-to-know-what-to-test)
  - [Kent C. Dodds - How to know what to test? (Youtube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ahrvE062Kv4&pp=ygUMa2VudCBjIGRvZGRz)
- [No mocks allowed: A testing discussion with Kent C. Dodds](https://www.software-engineering-unlocked.com/no-mocks-allowed/)
- [Why integration tests are better than unit tests with Kent C. Dodds](https://www.software-engineering-unlocked.com/double-down-integration-tests-kent-dodds/)

- [Effective snapshot testing](https://kentcdodds.com/blog/effective-snapshot-testing) : This one highlights issues like false negatives and the lack of clarity in test intention when relying on snapshots.
  > [Most developers, upon seeing a snapshot test fail, will sooner just nuke the snapshot and record a fresh passing one instead of agonizing over what broke it.](https://x.com/searls/status/919594505938112512?s=20)

Perhaps my most practical takeaway from this one was [snapshot diff](https://www.npmjs.com/package/snapshot-diff) - I can see how this could be a more useful way to make use of snapshot tests
