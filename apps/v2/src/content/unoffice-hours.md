[Matt Webb coined the term](https://interconnected.org/home/2020/09/24/unoffice_hours) in 2020, and the idea has stuck with me ever since: open a slice of your week, take meetings with whoever books a slot, no agenda required. The point isn't office hours in the academic sense. It's manufactured serendipity — the kind of conversation that only happens when two people agree to talk without a specific reason.

I've been wanting to do this for a while. Now I am.

[Book a 30-minute call](https://cal.com/guide) — pick a time that works for you. No need to email first.

## What it's good for

- A general catch-up. I'd love to hear what you're working on, even if we've never met.
- Code review, architecture questions, or a second pair of eyes on something you're building.
- Career stuff. Job hunting, freelancing, whether consulting is for you, what it's like working at a certain type of company.
- Music. I'm always down to talk crate-digging, curation, or whatever you're listening to.
- Side projects, indie hacking, or any half-baked idea you'd like to pressure-test.
- Or nothing in particular. Sometimes the best calls have no agenda at all.

## What it isn't

- A sales pitch. I'm not pitching services and I'm not looking to be pitched at.
- Career coaching. I can share what I've seen, but I'm not a coach.
- A guaranteed outcome. You'll get 30 minutes of honest attention, not a solution.

## A few notes

- 30 minutes, video call, on Google Meet.
- If something comes up and you can't make it, no stress — just cancel from the calendar invite.
- I'm in SAST (UTC+2), so times shown are local to me. Pick whatever slot works for you.
